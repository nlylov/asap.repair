How to Mount a TV in an NYC Apartment (Plaster, Brick & Concrete Walls)

Mounting a TV in a New York City apartment isn't the same as in a typical house. The walls are different, the buildings have rules, and getting it wrong can mean a $300 TV crashing to the floor.

The Biggest Mistake: Mounting Into Plaster Without the Right Anchors

Plaster walls look solid but crack easily and don't hold standard drywall anchors well. We've seen TV mounts pulled clean off plaster walls because the wrong hardware was used. For a 55"+ TV on plaster, you absolutely need to hit studs — not just wall anchors.

Modern stud finders often struggle with plaster because the density fools the sensor. This is one of the main reasons customers call us after a DIY attempt.

Building Rules in NYC Co-ops and Condos

Many NYC buildings require proof of insurance before any contractor does work. At Repair Asap, we carry a Certificate of Insurance (COI) and can name your building's management company as additionally insured. This is something many handymen from apps can't easily provide.

Hiding TV Cables in an NYC Apartment

  • Cable raceways: Surface-mounted plastic channels. Paintable, no drywall cutting. Best for rentals.
  • In-wall cable management: Cables run inside the wall. Completely hidden. Requires cutting drywall (check building rules first).

Should You DIY or Hire a Pro?

For modern drywall with wood studs and a TV under 50", confident DIY-ers can usually manage. But for older buildings, plaster, brick, concrete, or large TVs — professional mounting is worth every dollar. A fallen TV can injure someone and void your TV's warranty.
