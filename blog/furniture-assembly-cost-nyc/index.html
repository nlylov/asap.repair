Assembly costs in NYC are higher than national averages — mainly because of parking, traffic, building logistics (elevators, doormen, COI requirements), and higher cost of living.

Why Is NYC More Expensive Than Other Cities?

  • Parking & travel time: Handymen often pay $20–$40 in parking per job, especially in Manhattan and Brooklyn.
  • Building requirements: Many co-ops and condos require a Certificate of Insurance (COI), elevator reservations, and doorman coordination.
  • Elevator buildings: Getting large furniture to upper floors takes significantly longer than in a house.
  • Tight spaces: NYC apartments are often small, making assembly more challenging.

Tips to Save Money

  • Bundle jobs: Multiple items in one visit saves on the service call fee.
  • Have parts ready: Unbox furniture before the handyman arrives to save time.
  • Check for damage first: Inspect for missing pieces before scheduling.
  • Book weekday mornings: Easier parking, faster scheduling.
