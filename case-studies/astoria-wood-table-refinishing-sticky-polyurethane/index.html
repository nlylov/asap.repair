The problem

Soft, sticky polyurethane that kept taking marks

The table had already been coated by someone else before Repair Asap arrived, but the top coat never cured into a durable surface. The polyurethane stayed soft in places, felt tacky, and showed rings and pressure marks when cups or other objects were left on the table.

Because the surface needed controlled prep, sanding, stain work, and drying time, the technician brought the table to a workshop instead of attempting a rushed on-site patch.

Scope of work

  • Inspecting sticky and tacky polyurethane failure
  • Transporting the table to a workshop
  • Removing the old failed polyurethane coating
  • Sanding and preparing the wood surface
  • Selecting and approving a stain color with the client
  • Applying a new protective polyurethane finish
  • Allowing drying and curing time before return

Workshop process

Why this was not just another coat of finish

A sticky or tacky polyurethane problem usually cannot be solved by hiding it under a new coat. The failed coating has to be removed so the next stain and protective finish can bond properly.

The technician removed the old soft polyurethane, sanded and prepared the wood, confirmed a warmer stain color with the client, and — after stain drying time — applied a new protective polyurethane finish.

This project was completed over two days to allow stripping, stain drying, polyurethane application, and drying/curing time. Timelines vary by table size, coating condition, and finish system.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Why was the polyurethane sticky or tacky?

A polyurethane top coat can stay soft and tacky if it was applied too thick, over an incompatible or uncured product, in poor conditions, or never fully cured. Once that happens, the surface keeps taking marks and a new coat on top usually will not fix it.

Can you just add another coat over a sticky finish?

Usually no. The failed soft coating has to be removed first so the new stain and protective polyurethane can bond to a sound surface. On this table the old coating was stripped back before any new color or top coat went on.

How long does table refinishing take?

This table was completed over two days to allow stripping, stain drying, polyurethane application, and drying/curing time. Timelines vary by table size, the condition of the old coating, and the finish system used.
