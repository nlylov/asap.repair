Starting point

Bare ground first, then client drawings and prepared lumber

The first photos in the album are the true before condition: only the fenced dirt area was there, with no planter or bench installed yet.

The client provided the drawings, instructions, and prepared lumber. One technician matched the boards to the plan, set supports, fastened the walls, and corrected pieces that had been labeled or prepared incorrectly.

Scope of work

  • Document the bare-ground before condition
  • Review client drawings and supplied instructions
  • Sort labeled and prepared boards by section
  • Set support posts for the planter structure
  • Attach boards and assemble the flower bed walls
  • Rework boards where labels or prep did not match the plan
  • Install the built-in bench last
Before

Before: bare fenced dirt, no planter yet

The project started as an empty fenced dirt strip along the back of the yard, with no planter, flower bed, or bench in place.

Build sequence

Bare ground to final bench installation

Before: the yard was only a dirt area inside the fence, with no planter or bench yet.

Assembly started with supports and planter boards, following the client drawings and instructions.

Several prepared boards had to be adjusted or refit because labels/prep did not match the plan.

The bench was installed last by the same technician, and the best final views were selected from the last videos.

Process

Build sequence: supports, boards, and planter shell

Assembly progressed from setting supports and fastening boards to the planter shell standing around the soil before final cleanup.

Why it mattered
