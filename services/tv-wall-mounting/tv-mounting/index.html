Wall Types We Work With

Mounting on Different Wall Surfaces

1

Drywall with Wood Studs

The most common wall type in NYC apartments. We locate studs with electronic and magnetic finders, then anchor directly into the framing with lag bolts for maximum hold.

2

Plaster Walls

Common in pre-war NYC buildings. Plaster over lath requires careful drilling to avoid cracking. We use appropriate anchoring methods based on the wall's condition and backing.

3

Brick & Concrete

Masonry walls require hammer drilling and tapcon screws or concrete sleeve anchors. We bring the right bits and hardware for a secure mount on exposed brick or solid concrete.

4

Metal Studs & Other Surfaces

Metal studs and less common wall types may require toggle bolts or specialty hardware. Send us a photo of your wall and we'll confirm the best approach before scheduling.

Mount Styles

Choose the Right Mount Type

We install all three common mount styles. Not sure which one you need? We'll help you decide.

Fixed Mount

TV sits flush against the wall with a low profile. Best for clean aesthetics when viewing angle doesn't need adjustment.

Tilting Mount

Allows the TV to tilt downward. Ideal for above-fireplace installs or higher wall placements where you're looking up at the screen.

Full-Motion (Articulating)

Extends, swivels, and tilts. Best for corner mounting or rooms where you need to adjust viewing angle from different seating areas.

Clean Installation

Cable Management Options

Surface Cable Covers

Paintable raceways that run along the wall surface. Works on most wall types — drywall, plaster, brick, or concrete. Clean look without cutting into walls.

In-Wall Cable Routing

For drywall installations, we can route HDMI and low-voltage cables through the wall for a completely hidden look. Note: power cables require a proper outlet — if one isn't nearby, a licensed electrician may be needed.

Cord-Free Appearance

Whichever option you choose, the goal is the same — a clean, professional look with no visible wires hanging from the TV.

Common Questions

TV Mounting FAQ

How much does TV mounting cost in NYC?

Pricing depends on TV size, wall type, and mount style. A standard drywall mount for a 50–55" TV starts around $125–$180. Use our instant calculator above for a quick estimate — we'll confirm the exact flat-rate price before any work begins.

Can you mount a TV on a brick or concrete wall?

In many cases, yes. We work with drywall, plaster, brick, concrete, and metal studs. We bring masonry drill bits, tapcon screws, and heavy-duty anchors designed for each surface. Send us a photo of your wall so we can confirm the best approach.

Do you hide the cables?

We offer cable management options including in-wall routing for HDMI and low-voltage cables on drywall, and surface-mounted raceways for brick and concrete walls. Note: running power cables in-wall requires a proper outlet — if one isn't nearby, a licensed electrician may be needed.

Can you mount a TV above a fireplace?

In most cases, yes. We use tilting mounts for above-fireplace installs, verify clearance from heat sources, and route cables safely. Send us a photo of the fireplace wall so we can confirm it's suitable for mounting.

What type of mount should I get?

It depends on your setup. Fixed mounts are best for a flush, clean look. Tilting mounts work well for higher placements. Full-motion (articulating) mounts are ideal for corner installs or rooms where you watch from different angles. We can recommend the right mount based on your TV size and wall.

Do I need to buy the mount myself?

You can purchase your own mount or we can recommend one that fits your TV size and wall type. Just let us know your TV model and preferred mount style when you request a quote.

Can you mount a TV in a rental apartment?

TV mounting requires drilling into the wall, so we recommend checking your lease first. In many cases landlords allow standard mounting — the holes can be patched and painted when you move out.

Do you service all NYC boroughs?

Yes. We mount TVs across Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island. Same-day scheduling depends on availability and location.
