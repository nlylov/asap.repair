Finish Options

One Pillar Service, Several Premium Looks

We are not launching six thin pages for each finish. This page covers the main decorative plaster choices and helps you pick the right system for the space.

Tadelakt-Style Finish

A smooth, softly polished mineral look for bathrooms, powder rooms, niches, vanities, and spa-like walls. Best discussed with the wet-area assembly before any finish decision.

Lime Plaster & Lime Paint

Breathable, mineral-based finishes with subtle movement. Strong for living rooms, bedrooms, entryways, dining areas, and calm textured backdrops.

Marmorino / Venetian / Veneziano

Refined stone-like depth, from matte mineral movement to more polished decorative plaster. Good for feature walls, fireplaces, hospitality-style rooms, and high-impact entries.

Carrera-Style Plaster

A marble-inspired direction for clients who want soft veining, luminous surface variation, and a custom finish without installing full stone slabs.

Bathroom & Shower Walls

Possible when the surface build-up is appropriate. Wet-area projects require careful prep, waterproofing behind the finish, compatible sealers, and realistic maintenance expectations.

Accent Walls & Feature Areas

Bedrooms, living rooms, offices, fireplaces, headboard walls, corridors, and commercial feature walls where texture and craftsmanship should be visible.

Material Systems

Professional Application, System by System

Decorative plaster succeeds when the substrate, primer, plaster layers, compression, color, sealer, and maintenance plan work together. We may discuss products such as Vasari Lime Plaster & Paint when they fit the job, but the recommendation is not limited to one brand.

  • Surface review and prep plan before finish selection
  • Mineral plaster, lime paint, wax, soap, or sealer choices based on use
  • Sample-board review for texture, sheen, and color movement
  • Care instructions matched to the final finish system
Wet Areas

Bathrooms Need Extra Honesty

Decorative plaster can look beautiful in bathrooms, but the wall assembly matters more than the marketing name. Shower and wet-zone work is quoted only after reviewing the surface, waterproofing needs, edges, penetrations, and expected use.

Important: Wet-area finishes require proper waterproofing behind the decorative surface. Decorative plaster is a finish layer, not a substitute for structural waterproofing.

Process

How a Decorative Plaster Project Works

Room Review

Send photos, dimensions, target finish references, and any moisture concerns. We identify whether the surface is a good candidate.

Finish Direction

We narrow the finish family: Tadelakt-style, lime plaster, lime paint, Marmorino, Venetian, or Carrera-style.

Surface Prep Plan

We define patching, sanding, priming, substrate correction, wet-area detailing, and protection before finish work begins.

Sample / Approval

For premium finishes, a sample board helps confirm color, texture, sheen, and movement before full application.

Application

Layering, trowel work, compression, drying windows, and sealer or wax steps are handled according to the selected system.

Care Plan

You get practical notes for cleaning, resealing expectations, touch-ups, and what to avoid so the finish ages well.
